The 1990s

  • 1990: A researcher at CERN, Tim Berners-Lee, develops HTML, giving rise to the World Wide Web.
  • 1993: The Pentium microprocessor is created, allowing for more advanced graphics and music on PCs.
  • 1994: Many video games are released on PC taking advantage of Pentium technology, such as "Command and Conquer".
  • 1996: Sergey Brin and Larry Page design the Google search engine at Stanford University.
  • 1999: Wi-Fi is created, allowing users to connect to the Internet without wires.
